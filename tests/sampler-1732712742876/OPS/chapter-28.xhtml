28. Identification

Emily Stone’s voice was curt. “Can we meet at the Great Dome?”

“Hello, Ms. Stone. Good to hear from you so soon. We’re not far. How about five minutes?” Alex Mercer replied smoothly.

“Alone, Agent Mercer. Can you do that?”

He glanced at his partner, Jake Lin, who shrugged.

“Yes, if it helps. But I assure you, Agent Lin is as trustworthy as I am,” Alex said with a smile.

“It’s not Agent Lin that I’m bothered about. What I have to ask is for your ears only. Does this work for you?”

“Sure, Ms. Stone. See you in a few at the steps of the Great Dome.”

After the call ended, Jake shook his head. “If I didn’t know better, I’d think she’s asking you out for dinner.”

“Pretty women do that all the time, Jake.”

“Hard to believe, buddy. But honestly, is that a wise move?”

“Do you think it’s a trap? I can handle it.”

“It’s more that we’re chasing too many loose ends. Getting Hartman should’ve been easy, but he vanished. Finding him should be a piece of cake,” Jake gestured at the traffic camera.

Mercer answered soberly, “This case isn’t what it seems. Sometimes, you must pull at different ends to unravel a complex situation.”

Five minutes later, Emily and Alex stood before the Great Dome.

“Here I am, Ms. Stone. Shall we walk?” Mercer asked, looking over the green lawn of Killian Court, buzzing with students.

“Thank you, Agent Mercer,” Emily said, her eyes scanning the court.

“Why are you sending those goons after me and David? Intimidation won’t work,” she added, her voice tense.

“I don’t know what you’re talking about,” he replied, meeting her gaze.

Emily pulled out her phone. “Let me show you,” she said, opening her camera roll. Distant laughter drifted over the lawn, which smelled of freshly cut grass.

She watched him closely. Recognition flickered in his eyes.

“You know who those men are, Mr. Mercer, right?”

Alex studied her determined face. Sharing information could help her open up. Time was ticking to get Hartman, and Emily might be their only lead.

“Yes. They’re not FBI. That’s Captain Luke Brennan,” he said, zooming in on Brennan’s face.

“They’re special operators. Marines. I served with Brennan under General Clayton. He’s capable but not discreet.”

Emily’s eyes widened. She scribbled the names in her notebook. “Why is the military involved? Is that legal?”

“Good question, Ms. Stone. I have no idea. But they’re dangerous men.”

“Did they try to kidnap Joe?”

Mercer halted. “How do you know about that, Ms. Stone?”

“Why did you tell me about Brennan, Agent Mercer?”

“Because I want the truth. I want to know what really happened in Georgetown and why. I want justice for the victims. It could be Hartman or someone else. I don’t care about probabilities; I want facts.”

Emily studied his face, noting his agitation. She was relieved when she felt that his emotions were genuine. “I want the truth too, Agent Mercer. Yes, I spoke to Joe after he escaped those operators… and you. He’s flawed but not a cold-blooded killer.”

“But you won’t tell me where he is, will you?”

Emily watched a goldfinch fly by. “Call me Emily. I believe you want the truth, but I can’t tell you where he is. I’ll buy him all the time he needs to find it—the truth.”

“Call me Alex or just Mercer. The pressure is high, Emily. This is bigger than the Georgetown killings. It’s about trust in the system. Hartman doesn’t have much time. I feel the investigation is already settled, and the grand finale is near.”

“You mean presenting a solution rather than justice?” Emily trembled with anger.

“I’m afraid so. Please persuade Joe to talk to me.”
